# The Best Hikes in New Hampshire

I moved to New Hampshire from Washington DC at the end of June and plan on doing a good bit of exploring the hiking opportunities here. 
I plan on making this a living document while I live here and will update it as I try out new hikes. Please note hikes are listed in order I completed them and are not ranked.

## Franconia Ridge Trail (Loop)

Hiked on July 21, 2026. [Link to AllTrails](https://www.alltrails.com/trail/us/new-hampshire/mount-lafayette-and-franconia-ridge-trail-loop).

This is the first big hike I've done since moving up to NH. My favorite part was the couple mile stretch of trail on the ridge. This section of the trail gives an incredible 360-degree view and allows you to see mountains for miles 
and miles around you. Fortunately it was a beautiful day but it can be very windy and cold up top, so packing layers is important. About two-thirds of the way there is a lodge with bathrooms and as well as water refill which was clutch!

In terms of logisitcs, I did this hike on a Monday and started around 7:45 AM and finished in about 6 hours. It was pretty busy for a Monday; when we arrived the parking lot was already about half-full. The night before I camped at [Maple Haven Campgrounds](https://campmaplehaven.com/). I haven't done much camping but 
the campsite had what we needed, as well as access to a small river which was nice!
